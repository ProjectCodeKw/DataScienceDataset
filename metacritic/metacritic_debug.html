Insurgency: Sandstorm,stands out in the crowded field of first-person shooters with its intense and realistic depiction of modern combat. Launched as the sequel to the original Insurgency, Sandstorm enhances the franchises reputation for delivering gritty, tactical gameplay that rewards teamwork and strategy over lone-wolf **** of the most notable aspects of Insurgency: Sandstorm is its commitment to realism. The game eschews the more arcade-like elements found in many of its peers in favor of a more grounded approach. Weapons behave realistically, with significant recoil and authentic ballistics. This makes gunfights tense and deadly, where a single bullet can be fatal. The sound design further amplifies the immersion, with gunfire and explosions resonating with a visceral, bone-rattling intensity that places players right in the heart of the **** tactical gameplay is another strong point. Insurgency: Sandstorm requires players to communicate and coordinate effectively to succeed. Each match demands careful planning and precise execution, whether youre capturing objectives, defending positions, or escorting valuable assets. The games various modes, including Push, Firefight, and Checkpoint, offer diverse challenges that test different aspects of tactical proficiency.Visually, Insurgency: Sandstorm may not boast the cutting-edge graphics of some AAA titles, but it compensates with meticulously designed maps that offer a variety of environments, from dense urban centers to sprawling rural landscapes. These maps are not only visually distinct but also intricately designed to support a wide range of tactical approaches.However, Insurgency: Sandstorm is not without its flaws. New players may find the steep learning curve daunting, as the game does little to hold your hand. The realism that hardcore fans love can be a barrier to entry for those accustomed to more forgiving shooters. Additionally, while the game has seen numerous updates and content additions, it still suffers from occasional bugs and performance issues that can detract from the overall experience.Despite these drawbacks, Insurgency: Sandstorm offers a refreshing alternative to more mainstream shooters. Its emphasis on realism and tactical gameplay creates a uniquely intense experience that stands out in the genre. For players who crave a challenging and authentic depiction of modern combat, Insurgency: Sandstorm is a compelling choice that rewards patience, precision, and teamwork.

Sesm1c
Insurgency Sandstorm is my all time favorite first person shooter at the moment and I love every second I play. The game has AMAZING gunplay with a fast time to kill (TTK) and hitscan bullets only at super close ranges. Each and every gun have their own ballistics, say you wanted to try and snipe with an M4. Well good luck with that its going to have more bullet drop and won't one shot kill at far ranges like actual snipers. Want to use a shotgun with slugs or flechette rounds? Well get ready to use a shotgun that acts like a somewhat realistic shotgun with minimal spread at short ranges and long range potential. Every gun has a ton of attachments you can put on them to help with recoil (which this game has a lot of), reloading speed, aim down sights speed (ADS Speed), Bipods to make recoil and gun sway nonexistent when mounted, etc. The system used for weapons and attachments is amazing too with 15 points you have to spend wisely to fully pimp out your Security or Insurgent. you have to carefully pick what to spend your points on, want a better gun with a faster TTK than others due to caliber and rate of fire? Well get ready to use more points on that weapon than others which could mean you can't have as many attachments as you could on another gun or you can't use a secondary or grenades. This token mechanic helps balance the game out ass better weapons cost more making it so people are more reluctant to use that weapon as they would rather have a secondary and more attachments. I will say shotguns should cost more tokens as they are extremely cheap and you can fully kit one out with tokens to spare although no one really uses shotguns so I guess it doesn't really matter. Speaking of weapons the game offers a pretty big arsenal with both Security and Insurgents getting their own weapons. Security usually gets the more modern and newer versions of weapons such as M4A1s, G3s, Tavors, etc. while Insurgents get AKMs and 74s, WWII era guns, FALs, and things that in the real world are cheap to manufacture or were over manufactured and bought for cheap. Player customization is huge with you being able to customize nearly everything about your operator. Sadly Security has better skins for blending into your environment and some paid skins like a ghillie suit while insurgents don't get camos. The only good Insurgent skin for blending in is a paid one that only works in snow maps. This game does suffer from poor optimization but it has severely improved since launch with better servers and less bugs. The game runs fine and looks great on my R7 260 (I know it's a terrible card but its all I could find in 2021). I get around 60-70 frames on low to medium settings but that could be to having a lower resolution monitor. On bigger maps like summit it ends up crashing to about 20-40 frames which is a nightmare to play on in this game. Overall this game is amazing with a few issues that the devs are already working on trying to improve servers and optimization. They also keep adding weapons and maps in every new major update (not every single update only the big ones) and I can't wait to see where this game goes in the future.

(I played 'Insurgency: Sandstorm' for 29 hours until today and I came here after about 350 hours in Insurgency the 1st (2014), which was and still is awesome.) This is a solid game: not for everyone, but beautiful, tactical, rough, challenging, complete and varied. You have access to the main content of a respectful FPS: solo, coop and competitive modes, two teams, a bunch of different classes, a dozen maps. There are some major improvements compared to Insurgency (2014): free updates and events, some reinvented maps, a plethora of customizable items and characters (to buy with game money though), vehicules you can drive, truly immersive door kicking system, as well as the intimidation and the mask and gas systems. You have the opportunity to ask for air support to help you and your team progress. But these tactical nukes/smoke systems sometimes completely ruin the round. At some point they are inevitable and impossible to counter. You just have to pray you're not at the bad place at the wrong moment. In this perspective I could also write about AI which swings between sixth sense (bots can see through smoke and some walls) and incredible foolishness. Developpers added something looking like a kill cam but which proves to be particularly useless, especially in competitive mode. As I speak about competitive mode, be prepared to wait and do nothing for the half of the round because when you're dead, you're dead. One shot can make you out of the action for 5 complete minutes (in a FPS that is a tragedy) and that is not when you and your teammates get spawnkilled... Coop modes are full of campers who don't play the objectives and do their best to get more kills easily. The conclusion is that with a little practice, coop modes are way funnier to play (less death, more kills, more melee action and less time being out of the game). Sound effects were one of the greatest points of Insurgency (2014) but it feels like they have put less effort in it because some weapons sound like plastic and fake metal. It is also true for some animations and special effects: I mean the molotov flames are quite beautiful but it is clearly not suppposed to work that way. Bullets also seems to do way less damage (and it's surely linked to the fact that devs deleted armour-piercing bullets). It becomes really frustrating when you get in a gunfight and the opponent doesn't die after 3 of 4 bullets in the thorax. In this way you have to activate automatic and bursts gunshots because manual are not fast enough. Another defect is that the devs couldn't help themselves from adding some dumb DLC's with skins and gear sets. But all of this will not wipe out the main flaw of 'Insurgency: Sandstorm' which is according to me the loss of the Workshop in Steam, making impossible to easily add mods and packages. Definitively not worth its price unless you are here to play in immersive and beautiful environments. The most powerful experience I had with this game (and I recommend it to each and everyone) is to play while listening to Run The Jewels (their 3rd album to be precise). This album is perfect for the pace of 'Insurgency: Sandstorm', it is brutal, it is sharp and it is precise: a must-test!
